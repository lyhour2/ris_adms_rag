INTERNAL CREDIT POLICY v3.2
Document ID: ICP-3.2
Last reviewed: 2024-01

SECTION 1 — DEBT SERVICE TO INCOME (DSTI)
The DSTI ratio measures the proportion of a borrower's gross monthly income consumed by
total debt repayment obligations including the requested loan installment.

Policy rule 1.1: DSTI must not exceed 40% for standard personal loan and overdraft products.
Policy rule 1.2: DSTI between 35% and 40% requires additional scrutiny and must be escalated
to a senior credit analyst before final approval.
Policy rule 1.3: DSTI below 30% is considered within the acceptable standard range and does
not require additional commentary on this metric alone.
Policy rule 1.4: For mortgage products, DSTI ceiling is 38% given the longer-term commitment.

SECTION 2 — DEBT CEILING (DEBT/PLAFOND RATIO)
The Debt/Plafond ratio represents total outstanding debt as a proportion of the borrower's
approved credit plafond or total available credit.

Policy rule 2.1: Debt/Plafond ratio above 75% triggers mandatory manual review regardless
of other scores.
Policy rule 2.2: Debt/Plafond between 60% and 75% is considered moderate and should be
noted in the assessment but does not alone prevent approval.
Policy rule 2.3: Debt/Plafond below 60% is acceptable for standard products.

SECTION 3 — DEBT TO INCOME (MONTHS)
Debt/Income ratio in months represents how many months of gross income would be required
to repay all outstanding debts (excluding the requested loan).

Policy rule 3.1: Debt/Income ratio above 18 months is classified as high leverage.
High leverage cases require sign-off from a senior analyst.
Policy rule 3.2: Debt/Income between 12 and 18 months is moderate leverage — acceptable
for applicants with strong application and affordability scores.
Policy rule 3.3: Debt/Income below 12 months is low leverage and is considered positive.

SECTION 4 — PROFITABILITY (rNPV)
The Random Net Present Value (rNPV) represents the expected economic value of the loan
to the institution, accounting for probability of default, recovery rates, prepayment, and
cost of capital.

Policy rule 4.1: A positive rNPV mean is a minimum condition for loan approval under
standard credit policy.
Policy rule 4.2: Applications with negative rNPV mean may only be approved through the
Policy Exception Process (PEP), which requires documentation of strategic rationale.
Policy rule 4.3: rNPV mean above €500 is considered financially sound for personal loan
products up to €20,000.
Policy rule 4.4: The rNPV is computed as a random variable; the mean and standard deviation
at origination are both reported. High standard deviation relative to the mean indicates
significant uncertainty in the outcome.

SECTION 5 — DECISION MATRIX
Decisions are mapped to three outcome categories:
- GREEN (Approve): All key indicators within policy thresholds, positive rNPV, no rule flags.
- YELLOW (Manual Review): One or more indicators in borderline range, or minor rule flags.
  Requires credit analyst review before final decision.
- RED (Reject): One or more indicators exceed hard limits, negative rNPV, or multiple flags.
  Rejection must be logged and remains subject to applicant's right to human review.
