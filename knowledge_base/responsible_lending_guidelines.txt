RESPONSIBLE LENDING GUIDELINES
Document ID: RLG-2024
Applicable to: All retail credit products — personal loans, mortgages, overdrafts
Last reviewed: 2024-01

SECTION 1 — OVER-INDEBTEDNESS PREVENTION
Lenders have a legal and ethical obligation to assess whether the granting of credit places
the borrower at material risk of over-indebtedness.

Rule 1.1: Where DSTI exceeds 35% AND the annualised default probability is above 4%,
the application requires enhanced affordability assessment. The analyst must document
evidence that the borrower can sustain the obligation under stress conditions.

Rule 1.2: Where the combined effect of all outstanding obligations and the requested loan
would result in Debt/Income above 20 months, the lender must demonstrate that the
borrower has sufficient residual income for essential living expenses.

Rule 1.3: Lenders must not approve loans where there is clear evidence of existing
financial distress (e.g., current past-due amounts greater than two months' installment).

SECTION 2 — ALTERNATIVE STRUCTURES FOR BORDERLINE CASES
For borderline applications where the standard loan structure is not approvable but the
borrower has demonstrable creditworthiness, lenders should consider:

Rule 2.1: Reduced loan amount — if a lower amount brings DSTI or rNPV into acceptable
range, the analyst should propose this as an alternative before issuing a rejection.

Rule 2.2: Extended term — increasing the repayment period reduces monthly installment
and therefore DSTI. This may be appropriate where long-term default probability is low.

Rule 2.3: Phased disbursement — for larger amounts, releasing funds in tranches
conditional on repayment behavior may be considered.

SECTION 3 — VULNERABLE BORROWERS
Applications from borrowers showing indicators of financial vulnerability (recent job change,
high medical expenses, recent divorce/separation inferred from income changes) must be
handled with additional care. Automatic rejection is not appropriate; these cases must be
referred to a specialist team.

SECTION 4 — DOCUMENTATION OBLIGATIONS
Every credit decision must be documented with:
- The specific indicators and thresholds applied
- The rationale for the outcome
- Reference to the policy or guideline sections relied upon
- Identity of the analyst who reviewed the decision (for yellow and red outcomes)
